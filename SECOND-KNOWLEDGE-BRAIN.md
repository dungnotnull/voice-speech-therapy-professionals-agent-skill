## 1. Core Concepts & Frameworks

### 1.1 Vocal Health & Speech-Language Pathology for Voice Professionals — Foundational Methods

### 1.1 Anatomy & physiology
Larynx, vocal folds (lamina propria layers), subglottic pressure, myoelastic-aerodynamic theory; respiration (diaphragm), resonance (vocal tract formants). Registers (chest/mixed/head/falsetto), Estill figures (true/false vocal fold, thyroid tilt, etc.).
### 1.2 Voice dimensions
Pitch (Hz), loudness (dB SPL), quality (jitter, shimmer, HNR), resonance, breathiness/strain. Articulation: place/manner/voicing; intelligibility.
### 1.3 Hygiene & injury
Hydration, vocal rest, avoid clearing/smoking, manage laryngopharyngeal reflux, pacing; vocal load management. Overuse → fatigue, edema, nodules/polyps, muscle tension dysphonia (MTD). Red flags: persistent hoarseness >2-3 weeks → SLP/ENT referral.
### 1.4 Exercises
Warm-up (glides, lips/tongue trills), breath support (diaphragmatic), resonance (forward focus), Estill figures, articulation drills (tongue twisters, pacing), cool-down. Progressive overload; record-keep.
